# Day 3 — Verification Chain Report

## Overview
This exercise validated 5 AI-generated placement statistics using a 3-step verification chain:
1. Gemini-generated claims
2. Perplexity cross-check
3. Primary source validation

At least one claim required a FALSE/PARTIAL verdict, which was achieved.

---

## Verification Matrix Summary

### 1. IIT Bombay CSE Highest Package (₹3.67 crore in 2026)
- **Verdict:** ❌ FALSE  
- **Issue:** Misattributed year and specialization  
- **Insight:** ₹3.67 crore belongs to earlier international offer cycle, not 2026 CSE placements  

---

### 2. BITS Pilani Placement Rate (95% in 2025–26)
- **Verdict:** ❌ FALSE  
- **Issue:** Inflated statistic  
- **Insight:** Actual interim placement rate ~80%, no official 95% confirmation  

---

### 3. JNTUH B.Tech Placement Rate (62% in 2025)
- **Verdict:** ✅ TRUE  
- **Insight:** 312 out of 501 students placed → 62% confirmed  

---

### 4. IIIT Hyderabad CSE Average Salary (₹32.8 LPA)
- **Verdict:** ❌ FALSE  
- **Issue:** Program/year conflation  
- **Insight:** Actual CSE average ~₹31.98 LPA in latest completed cycle  

---

### 5. IIT Kanpur Day 1 Offers (672 in 2025–26)
- **Verdict:** ✅ TRUE  
- **Insight:** Official SPO announcement confirms 672 Day 1 offers  

---

## Reflection

The claim that looked most authoritative but was actually weakest was Claim #4: "IIIT Hyderabad CSE average salary ₹32.8 LPA in 2025–2026." The figure appeared consistent across secondary summaries, which initially increased confidence. However, primary placement data showed the correct value was closer to ₹31.98 LPA for the latest completed cycle, and the ₹32.8 LPA figure likely belonged to a different program or dataset mix. This demonstrates that even highly specific and confident numerical claims generated by AI systems can be subtly incorrect, and only verification against primary sources ensures reliability.

---

## Key Learning
- Confidence ≠ correctness  
- Perplexity ≠ final authority  
- Primary source verification is mandatory
