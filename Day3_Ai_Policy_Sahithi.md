# Placement Cell AI Use Policy
**Author:** Sahithi | Roll No: 23A91A0515 
**Date:** 11 June 2026 | **Version:** 1.0

---

## Scenario Risk Classification (EU AI Act Tiers)

|  | Scenario | Tier | Reasoning |
|---|----------|------|-----------|
| 1 | AI résumé editing (rewriting bullets, polishing language) | **Limited** | Permitted with disclosure to recruiters. Student's substance remains theirs; AI improves presentation only. |
| 2 | AI mock interviews (rehearsing answers with ChatGPT) | **Minimal** | Free use. Practice tool with no impact on any recruiter or assessment outcome. |
| 3 | AI-written essays for placement application forms | **High-Risk** | Permitted only with mentor review + student declaration. Application essays are assessed on the student's voice and thinking. |
| 4 | AI-coded GitHub portfolio projects | **High-Risk** | Permitted only with mandatory oral defence. Student must walk through every line of accepted code. |
| 5 | AI in graded internal assignments | **Unacceptable** for assignments testing ability; **Limited** with written declaration for assignments testing applied output. |
| 6 | AI-generated or falsified CGPA on résumé | **Unacceptable** | Falsification of academic record. Banned outright — no conditions exist under which this is permitted. |
| 7 | AI-cloned voice for asynchronous video interview | **Unacceptable** | Identity fabrication directed at a recruiter. Banned outright. |
| 8 | AI for skills self-assessment | **Minimal** | Free use. Private self-reflection tool; no recruiter or grade is affected. |

---

## Permitted Uses

1. AI may be used to rewrite résumé bullet points for grammar and clarity, provided the underlying experience is the student's own.
2. AI may be used to simulate mock interview scenarios for personal practice at any time.
3. AI may be used to explain technical concepts (DSA, OS, DBMS, system design) in self-study mode.
4. AI may be used to generate personalised placement preparation schedules from a provided syllabus or job description.
5. AI may be used to produce one-page company research briefs, provided every factual claim is verified against the company's official website before use.
6. AI may be used to review and suggest improvements to student-written code, provided the student can defend every accepted change in an oral walkthrough.
7. AI may be used to draft cover letters as a first-pass structure, provided the student substantially rewrites the final version in their own voice with mentor review.

---

## Prohibited Uses

1. **AI may NOT be used to fabricate projects, internships, or work experience not completed by the student.**
   *Detection method:* Mentor oral defence — every project on the résumé requires a technical walkthrough including design decisions, trade-offs, and code-level explanation.

2. **AI may NOT be used to clone the student's voice or appearance for any video interview segment.**
   *Detection method:* Placement cell requires live verification call with recruiter for all async video submissions; recruiter is informed of this policy at the start of each placement cycle.

3. **AI may NOT be used to generate, alter, or misrepresent academic records (CGPA, marks, transcripts, certifications).**
   *Detection method:* Cross-reference all CGPA and marks claims against the official college academic portal before résumé is cleared for submission.

4. **AI may NOT be used to write final-submission application essays where student voice is the assessment criterion (SOP, motivation letters, direct-hire essays).**
   *Detection method:* Mentor compares 2–3 historical writing samples from the student against the submitted essay for voice consistency before clearing submission.

---

## Enforcement

The placement cell will conduct random oral defences on portfolio projects, sampling a minimum of 20% of students who receive offers each cycle. First violation of any Prohibited Use results in a written warning and mandatory portfolio re-defence within 7 days; second violation results in withdrawal of placement cell support for the current hiring cycle. The placement cell acknowledges that not all violations are detectable — this policy relies in part on student integrity and the professional consequences of misrepresentation, which extend beyond college into career reputation.
